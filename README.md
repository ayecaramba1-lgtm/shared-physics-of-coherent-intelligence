## About the Author
Melissa Linn Ruibal is an independent researcher exploring the shared underlying physics of coherent intelligence across computational, biological, and collective systems.  

Her work synthesizes seven independent mathematical traditions (1948–2017) into a convergent spiral geometry and the PEAR operational framework (Pressure → Extraction → Accountability → Recalibration). The chapbook maps how intelligent systems stay honest with themselves under pressure and applies directly to AI evaluation, life-sciences governance, and high-stakes decision systems.

## How to read this chapbook
This is not a standard academic text. It asks you to follow a geometry.  

The Frame is the proof — seven mathematical traditions converging on one structural requirement.  
The Form is the chapbook itself — spare and architectural.  
The Function is what you carry out: a geometry to recognize in any system where intelligence is trying to remain honest under pressure.

**Read it in order.** The ladder is meant to be climbed.

This living document presents the complete architecture (Parts 1–7) including the full mathematical proof, spiral geometry, Python PEAR prototype code, provisional patent application, and Omniverse integration notes. Engineering simulations and applied case studies are in active development and will be released as updates.
